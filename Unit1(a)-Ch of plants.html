1.1 Characteristics of plants

Angiossperm are plants that have flowers and their reproduction is done by flowers. All flowering plants produce seeds. And it is covered by a fruit. Angiosperms have complicated flowers with complicated structure. These are the most develop plants in the world. There are 2 types of Angiosperms
(1) Dicotyledonnous Plants
(2) Monocotyledonous Plants

Monocotyledon have one cotyledon (seed leaf), which is the food store of a seed. They also have leaves with parallel veins, and flower parts of multiples of three.
Dicotyledons have two cotyledons, leaves with branching, netlike veins, and flower parts in group of four or five.

Five common characteristics of seed plants in Homes

  1. Leaves

    Seed plants all possess leaves in some pattern and configuration. Leaves vary widely in appearance and can be very small or large. Leaves are part of the shoot system of the plants that is located above ground. It is where the photosynthesis, the conversion of light into sugar, take place. Food is also stored in leaves and release as it is needed by the plant's cell.

  2. Stem

    Seed plants all have stems to help them support the plant and hold the cells for photosynthesis, food and water transport, as well for reproduction. Stems are where flowers are located, which then turn into seeds that create more plants. Not all plants have visible flowers. Some flowers are very inconspicious, but serve the same function as seed dispersal as more lush flowers.
